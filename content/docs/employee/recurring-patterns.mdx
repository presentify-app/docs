---
title: Recurring Patterns
description: Set up repeating weekly presence schedules that fill your planner automatically.
---

Recurring patterns let you define a weekly schedule that repeats automatically. Instead of setting your status manually every week, create a pattern and let Presentify fill in your planner.

## Creating a pattern

1. Navigate to **Presence** in the sidebar
2. In the **Recurring Patterns** card, click **Add Pattern**
3. Configure the pattern:
   - **Weekdays** — Select which days of the week (e.g., Monday, Wednesday, Friday)
   - **Status** — Choose a presence status (In Office, Remote, etc.)
   - **Location** — Optionally select an office location
   - **Repeat interval** — Every week, every 2 weeks, every 3 weeks, or every 4 weeks
   - **End date** — Optionally set when the pattern should stop
4. Click **Save**

## Managing patterns

All your patterns are listed in the Recurring Patterns card on the Presence page.

For each pattern you can:

- **Toggle on/off** — Use the switch to temporarily disable a pattern without deleting it
- **Edit** — Change the weekdays, status, location, or interval
- **Delete** — Remove the pattern permanently

## Applying patterns

Patterns are projections — they show what your schedule would look like, but don't create actual presence entries until applied.

Days filled by patterns appear with a **dashed border** in the week grid, indicating they are projected (not yet committed).

Click **Apply Patterns** to convert projected days into actual presence entries for upcoming dates.

## How patterns interact with manual entries

- If you manually set a status for a day that also has a pattern, your manual entry takes priority.
- Patterns only fill days that don't already have a presence entry.
- Disabling a pattern does not remove entries that were already applied.

## Example

A typical hybrid work pattern:

- **Monday, Wednesday, Friday** → In Office at "Berlin HQ", every week
- **Tuesday, Thursday** → Remote, every week

This gives you a predictable schedule that colleagues can see in the team calendar.
