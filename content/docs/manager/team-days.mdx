title: Team Days
description: Set up recurring office days for your team to encourage in-person collaboration.
---

Team Days are recurring office days that you set for your team. They help establish a rhythm — for example, "the team meets in the office every Tuesday and Thursday."

> Team Days require a **Pro** plan. Managers, Office Admins, Admins, and Owners can create and manage team days. Members can view them.

## Creating a team day

1. Navigate to **Team** in the sidebar
2. Scroll to the **Team Day Planner** card
3. Click **Add Team Day**
4. Configure:
   - **Day of week** (required) — Monday through Friday
   - **Description** (optional) — e.g., "Sprint planning" or "Team sync" (max 200 characters)
   - **Preferred location** (optional) — Select an office location
   - **Minimum attendees** (optional) — Minimum number of team members expected in office
5. Click **Save**

You can create one team day per weekday. Attempting to create a second team day for the same weekday will show an error.

## Managing team days

For each team day you can:

- **Toggle on/off** — Temporarily disable without deleting. Inactive team days appear grayed out.
- **Edit** — Change the day, description, location, or minimum attendees
- **Delete** — Remove permanently

## How team days appear

Team days are visible across Presentify:

- **Dashboard** — The weekly overview highlights team days with a violet border and "Team Day" label
- **Presence Planner** — Team day badges appear on the corresponding weekday
- **Team Calendar** — Team days are marked with a violet border

## Upcoming Team Days

Below the Team Day Planner, the **Upcoming Team Days** section shows the next 7 calendar days that match your configured team days. For each:

- Date and description
- **Attendance progress** — How many team members are planned in office vs. total
- **Minimum met** indicator — Green if the minimum attendees target is met, amber if not

A **Best Day** suggestion may appear, based on historical attendance data, highlighting the weekday with the highest typical in-office coverage.
